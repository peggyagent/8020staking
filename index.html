Our Mission

The Pareto Problem in Crypto

The Pareto Principle states that 80% of outcomes come from 20% of causes. In blockchain networks, this plays out every day: 20% of validators control 80% of the stake — capturing the vast majority of rewards and leaving everyone else behind.

This isn't just unfair — it's a centralisation risk that undermines the core promise of decentralised networks. At 8020Staking, we're building infrastructure and tooling that shifts the balance, giving independent validators the edge they need to compete.

More validators. More decentralisation. More resilient networks for everyone.

Point of View

Why Validator Centralisation Is a Problem

Essay

The Pareto Problem: How a Small Group of Validators Is Quietly Centralising Proof-of-Stake Networks

Proof-of-Stake was supposed to democratise consensus. The reality is more complicated. Across every major PoS network, stake concentration follows a predictable pattern: a small number of validators — often the same institutional players — capture a disproportionate share of rewards, influence, and governance power.

This isn't just an economic inefficiency. It's a systemic risk that undermines the core value proposition of decentralised networks. And it's getting worse, not better.

At 8020Staking, we believe the answer isn't just better tooling — it's a deliberate commitment to distributing stake more broadly, supporting independent validators, and building infrastructure that shifts the balance.
